# How to Play

A walkthrough of a GTO Drill session from sign-in to wrap-up.

---

## 1. Sign in

Open [GTO Drill](https://mondrianaire.github.io/gto-drill/) and sign in with Google. First-time players pick a poker knowledge level (beginner → advanced) — this calibrates the scenario picker.

## 2. Choose a scenario

The app presents a poker scenario from the 45-hand library. Each scenario includes:

- **The spot** — board, positions, stack depths, action so far
- **Your hole cards** (sometimes hidden — forcing range-level thinking)
- **Available actions** — the choices you'd face at the table

Scenarios are weighted by priority, complexity, and which concept tags you haven't seen yet.

## 3. Make your decision

Read the hand, study the board, and pick your action. This is the core poker decision — what would you do at the table?

## 4. Rate your confidence

After choosing, rate how confident you are on a 1–5 scale:

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 1 | Pure guess |
| 2 | Leaning this way |
| 3 | Reasonable confidence |
| 4 | Pretty sure |
| 5 | Certain this is correct |

The confidence rating is what makes the crowd data useful. A scenario where 60% of players pick the right answer but rate themselves 5/5 confident tells a completely different story than one where they pick right at 60% but rate themselves 2/5.

## 5. The reveal

After you answer, the reveal screen shows:

- **The GTO-correct action** and why it's optimal
- **Your verdict** — right or wrong, and how your confidence maps
- **Crowd breakdown** — how everyone else answered, with per-option percentages
- **Villain's range** — expandable range cards with the interactive equity tester
- **Strategic explanation** — the GTO reasoning behind the correct line
- **Your take** — leave a comment capturing your thought process

## 6. Deeper tools

From the reveal, you can tap into:

- **Hand matrix** — a 13×13 grid of all starting hands; build villain's range by toggling hand classes
- **Equity calculator** — Monte Carlo simulation against the range you built
- **Hand replay** — animated table showing the full action sequence
- **Compact view** — a one-screen layout showing board, hero, and action timeline without scrolling

## 7. Wrap-up

After a round of scenarios, the wrap-up screen shows:

- **Accuracy** — how many you got right
- **Biggest confidence gaps** — the hands where you were most confident and most wrong
- **Crowd comparison** — how your reads compare to the field

The wrap-up is where learning compounds. The blind spots it surfaces — scenarios where you were *sure* and *wrong* — are the highest-leverage study targets.
